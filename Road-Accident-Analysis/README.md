## 📊 Project Overview
This project presents an **interactive Road Accident Analysis Dashboard** built using **Microsoft Excel** (Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, and Slicers). The dashboard analyzes **896,568 road accident casualties** across multiple dimensions such as severity, vehicle type, road type, weather conditions, lighting, location (urban/rural), and time trends (2019–2022).

The objective of this project is to **identify high-risk patterns**, **extract actionable insights**, and **demonstrate real-world data analysis skills** aligned with a professional Data Analyst role.

---

## 🎯 Business Questions Addressed
- Which accident severity contributes the most to total casualties?
- Which vehicle types are involved in the highest number of accidents?
- Are accidents more frequent in urban or rural areas?
- How do lighting and weather conditions affect accidents?
- Which road types are most dangerous?
- Are there any monthly or yearly accident trends?

---

## 📂 Dataset Summary
- **Time Period:** 2019–2022  
- **Total Casualties:** 896,568  
- **Key Dimensions:**
  - Accident Severity (Fatal, Serious, Slight)
  - Vehicle Type
  - Road Type
  - Weather Conditions
  - Light Conditions (Daylight / Darkness)
  - Urban vs Rural Areas
  - Month & Year

## 📈 Key Insights

### 1️⃣ Accident Severity
- **Slight injuries account for ~84%** of total casualties.
- **Fatal accidents contribute only ~1.8%**, but have the highest social impact.

**Insight:**  
High-frequency minor accidents require preventive measures, while fatal accidents need targeted interventions.

---

### 2️⃣ Vehicle-wise Casualties
- **Cars contribute ~77.5%** of total casualties.
- Bikes and goods vans follow at a significantly lower scale.

**Insight:**  
Road safety efforts should primarily focus on car drivers through stricter enforcement and awareness campaigns.

---

### 3️⃣ Road Type Risk
- **Single carriageways account for ~74%** of casualties.
- Dual carriageways and roundabouts show relatively fewer accidents.

**Insight:**  
Infrastructure improvements on single carriageways can significantly reduce accidents.

---

### 4️⃣ Urban vs Rural Analysis
- **Urban areas contribute ~61%** of total casualties.

**Insight:**  
Higher traffic density in cities demands better traffic management and pedestrian safety planning.

---

### 5️⃣ Light Conditions
- **72% of accidents occur in daylight**.

**Insight:**  
Accidents are driven more by traffic volume and human behavior than visibility issues alone.

---

### 6️⃣ Weather Conditions
- **Dry road conditions still show the highest number of casualties**.

**Insight:**  
Drivers tend to overspeed and take more risks during favorable weather conditions.

---

### 7️⃣ Time Trend Analysis
- Accident peaks are observed between **May and November**.
- A noticeable decline appears in **2022**, possibly due to policy changes or reduced mobility.

**Insight:**  
Seasonal road safety campaigns can be planned around high-risk months.

---

## ✅ Recommendations
- 🚦 Improve safety infrastructure on **single carriageways**
- 🚗 Launch **car-focused safety awareness programs**
- 🏙 Enhance **urban traffic monitoring and control**
- 🌞 Address **driver overconfidence** during daylight and dry conditions
- 📅 Implement **seasonal enforcement strategies** during peak months
