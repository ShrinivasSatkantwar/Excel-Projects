# 📊 Campaign Performance Analysis for New Customer Growth

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes a multi-channel marketing campaign to identify the most effective **campaign and channel combination** for driving **new customer growth**.

The analysis focuses on understanding performance across:
- Campaign types (A: Informational, B: Promotional)
- Channels (Email, Instagram, Web Banner)
- Customer segments (New vs Existing)

---

## 🎯 Business Objective
Determine:
> Which campaign + channel combination should be prioritized to maximize **new customer acquisition and revenue**

---

## 📂 Dataset Description
The dataset contains:
- Interaction ID
- Campaign Type (A/B)
- Channel (Email, Instagram, Web Banner)
- Customer Type (New/Existing)
- Converted (1 = Yes, 0 = No)
- Time on Site (seconds)
- Sales ($)

---

## 📊 Key Metrics
- **Conversion Rate** = Conversions / Total Users  
- **Revenue** = Sum of Sales  
- **New Customer Revenue** = Revenue from new users  
- **Customer Acquisition** = Number of new converted users  

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

- 📧 **Email** is the strongest channel across both campaigns  
- 🚀 **Campaign B** consistently delivers higher conversion rates  
- 💰 **Campaign A** generates higher revenue per customer  
- ⚖️ There is a clear trade-off:
  - Campaign B → Higher volume (scale)
  - Campaign A → Higher value (revenue per user)

---

## 💡 Recommendation

- ✅ Double down on **Email + Campaign B** for scalable customer acquisition  
- ✅ Use **Email + Campaign A** to maximize revenue per customer  
- 📈 Use Instagram for awareness and engagement  
- ❌ Reduce investment in Web Banner due to low performance  

---

## 🧪 Validation Strategy (A/B Testing)

To validate the recommendation:
- Split users into two groups:
  - Group A → Email + Campaign A
  - Group B → Email + Campaign B
- Track:
  - Conversion rate
  - New customers acquired
  - Revenue
  - Retention

---

## 📈 Outcome

The analysis shows that:
> The “best” campaign depends on the business goal — whether the focus is on **growth (scale)** or **revenue (value)**

---

## 🧠 Key Learning

This project highlights that strong data analysis is not about finding one best option, but about:
- Understanding trade-offs  
- Aligning decisions with business goals  
- Communicating insights clearly
